In this episode, a list of albums I found and listened to in 2025.

It does not contain exclusively albums published this year, much like the same thing I did for my favourite songs of 2025.

For every album, there will be a one-line comment that (hopefully) will make you listen to that song.

Enjoy!

First, some albums that you probably already know, if you follow this newsletter.

- SABLE, fABLE, by Bon Iver. Listen it here. Read it here.

- A Study of Losses, by Beirut. Listen it here. Read it here.

- Singles, by Future Islands. Listen it here. Read it here.

Next, some "new" records (at least for me)!

(They are not presented in any particular order.)

Captured Spirits,

by Mammal Hands

(published in 2020 with Gondwana Records).

this record will grow on you. it will literally capture your spirit .

Songs of the Earth (Instrumentals),

by David Longstreth / Dirty Projectors / s t a r g a z e

(published in 2025 by David Longstreth with Transgressive Records).

a soundtrack for our planet. I hope it will not become a eulogy soon.

River Calls,

by Message to Bears

(published in 2025 by Lost Birds).

a hidden gem. every listening session is a warm embrace.

Folk Traffic,

by Pietro Paris ( with Andrea Beninati, Ruggero Fornari & Francesco Panconesi)

(published in 2025 by Encore Music).

it started my jazz-era in this 2025. sensational.

Phantom Brickworks,

by Bibio

(published in 2024 by Warp Records).

listening to this record is trespassing a wall in reality. in the end you come back transformed.

Live Vol. 1,

by Parcels

(published in 2020 by Kitsuné Musique, with Because Music).

a live studio album with great energy, a fine record.

Whatever the Weather II,

by Whatever the Weather

(published in 2025 by Ghostly International).

a thermometer for your soul. you will find something floating in the air.

ATOMOS VII,

by A Winged Victory For The Sullen & Adam Wiltzie

(published in 2023 by Artificial Pinearch Manufacturing).

drone music, it will bring you a sudden, discomfortable peace.

People Watching,

by Sam Fender

(published in 2025 by Sam Fender, with Polydor Records).

maybe a hit? anyway, he does not feel like coming from this age - in a good sense.

Music For a Cosmic Garden,

by Takashi Kokubo & Andrea Esperti

(published in 2023 by WRWTFWW).

had something in mind for this, then I read the title tracks again: wonderful.

USA,

by Petey USA

(published in 2023 by Capitol Records, with Crow Island, and UMG Recordings).

a solid record which contains some pretty cool songs. liminal is a beautiful word.

The Disintegration Loops,

by William Basinski

(published in 2002 by Temporary Residence).

it will shred you to pieces. is each and single one of us nothing but temporary?
