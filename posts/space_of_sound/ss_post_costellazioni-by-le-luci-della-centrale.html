It is not easy to talk about this record, and at the same time it’s all so easy. It’s talking about something you recognize, but is nonetheless foreign to you. It’s not yours, but it’s in every place of your memory; you’ve listened to it so much that you feel a part of you is also defined in relation to it. It’s the record you return to, that you leave before departing, that you find again when you return, from which you distance yourself when you distance yourself from everything. It contains everything and all the rest it doesn’t yet contain, it is the haven of what you have lived that makes room for what you have not yet experienced. It contains places where you have already been but which you visit to connect them to this sound map.

Sound constellation .

The songs within it are stars, which create constellations if you look at them from a specific point - Earth, life: they are not necessarily there to form those symbols, but your perspective gives you those meanings.

You create them, the artist creates them, they are different, they are the same.

Le Luci della Centrale Elettrica is Vasco Brondi, and it was his project from 2007 to 2019; now he continues to tell stories producing music with his own name, after having abandoned the name of the project with a farewell concert tour - even though every now and then he lights one up in individual concerts, and these small events remain there to form a memory.

Time constellation .

dammi solo quello che mi disorienta

La Terra, l’Emilia, la Luna opens the record, and it already seems like night, with the fireflies, crickets, grasshoppers (?), among the fields between Ferrara and the Moon. Apart from this, there is silence tonight (c’è silenzio stasera) . Under a silver sky (cielo d ’argento ), a sky that covers the night, we have coordinates: a railway , a recently built mosque , and then a church , a castle , a road ; we have music that disorients the silence, and that music comes from a machine, or from North Africa , or from Eastern Europe , or from a machine in one of these two places so far away. We are in a place between the Moon and Northern Italy, we might as well be alone with the memory of the earth, Emilia and the Moon elsewhere, in Poland, in Krakow . There are two torn (strappati) hearts, thank goodness they’re not ours. There is a monument that remembers the suffering that men are capable of inflicting on other men. We promise not to tear out our hearts, but to safeguard them.

It’s a song of geographical references, of places like pins on a map, of memories and relationships and connections like a thread linking those points. It’s a surface without a center, it has no direction, and so we search for a meaning, a direction: we create a new theory of gravity, the first version of this theory is only momentary , for now it’s enough for us.

We are every place we fly to, every place we visit: we let ourselves be disoriented, we only want what disorients us, we are the action of desperation and the thought of hope. We pray, if we do, for all of this, to arrive in Germany , in Turkey , in Greece , driving in shifts and resting in shifts, a chant to doze off (appisolarci) , water to cross our deserts - and how much water is truly needed, by those who traverse real deserts!

We want the means to lift ourselves above them, to make them sufficient so that we die.
