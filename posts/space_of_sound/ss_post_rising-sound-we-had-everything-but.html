We Had Everything But Time , the new EP by Michael Chau ’s new project The Field Index, has come out on November 14th, 2025.

We already talked about the single released last month, and in that occasion I promised to deliver some thoughts on the EP. Here they are.

here the playlist on Apple Music.

The record starts with Catch , which reminds me of something coming from Beach House.

The drums are entertaining, flowing through during the song, along with a line of bass, and I’m already there, but anyway " take me with you when you disappear ”.

Trouble in Mind is a silhouette, like the first word of the song itself states. It is a recurring theme for me with this record, you feel like waves, the ambience (we’ll return to that with the last song) Chau creates embodies you, it gets to know you and then it transcends into something that could have come from a distant star, reaching on to you because you’ve been chosen.

And that’s just by listening one record, so you have to give them that.

The song fades, and another starts playing.

Cannibals is a song about “ something else ”, and the lyrics make their way through the music, the music is like heavy curtains and Chau’s voice appears to come behind them. The music has a rhythm that the lyrics can’t sustain, and at some point the lyrics end and all you’re left with is a drapery of sound, and you can’t peek through the fabric.

At that point, the song ends.

Chau comes from ambient and soundscape music, and My Inner Goth Girl in the first second gives you that; then, it transforms into 80s music in the way someone actually living in the 2020s would make it: you get the synths, and the drums, and the chords, in this order, plus a delicate voice, calling you to answer to one liminal phone call - in this times of social networks on a phone that does not ring anymore - and you’re all set.

Press photo of Michael Chau. Credits here.

Never “ surrender to the emptiness ”, because this single is full of surprised: it seems an undoing, a stripping of clothes to reveal who you really are after a run, to save yourself or maybe just to sweat a bit.

Nonetheless, “ The past is done, release the harm / we made it out alive ”, and this is all it matters. The songs builds on itself, it grows and becomes larger, the instruments make waves, they create an ocean of sound, Chau’s voice surfs on it, it reaches you from multiple places, it makes you yearn for more.

I said of My Inner Goth Girl that it is a good song if you like night calls, or even that bright part of the sunset just before sun goes off in the west.

It seems that is the mood of the whole record, and in cold November days it create an atmosphere you can feel, you can touch with your hands and breath in with every inhale.

If we’d listen to it, we ’d have everything - including time .
