<div class="pullquote"><p><strong>it is a space I no longer inhabit, nor want to inhabit. Youth Lagoon is complete. I’ve reached the top of a mountain, only to then be able to see a much larger one I want to ascend</strong></p></div><p><a href="https://youthlagoon.bandcamp.com/album/the-year-of-hibernation">17</a> came from a complete project: it’s the fourth track on the first album produced by <strong>Trevor Powers</strong> under the moniker <strong><a href="https://trevorpowe.rs/">Youth Lagoon</a></strong>, <em>The Year of Hibernation (2011). </em>The LP was followed by <em>Wondrous Bughouse (2013)</em> and <em>Savage Hills Ballroom (2015)</em>.</p><iframe class="spotify-wrap" data-attrs="{&quot;image&quot;:&quot;https://i.scdn.co/image/ab67616d0000b2733ef1207895b314d646f6a3b7&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;17&quot;,&quot;subtitle&quot;:&quot;Youth Lagoon&quot;,&quot;description&quot;:&quot;&quot;,&quot;url&quot;:&quot;https://open.spotify.com/track/5UVBumEwdUnzvqxrXOYLFA&quot;,&quot;belowTheFold&quot;:false,&quot;noScroll&quot;:false}" src="https://open.spotify.com/embed/track/5UVBumEwdUnzvqxrXOYLFA" frameborder="0" gesture="media" allowfullscreen="true" allow="encrypted-media" data-component-name="Spotify2ToDOM"></iframe><p>Powers sings that the day you stop imagining is the day you die (or at least this is what his mother told him): should this be connected to the publication of two new LPs since 2023, eight years after the statement that ended Youth Lagoon? </p><p>It’s not why we’re here though - let’s not speak of the future.</p><div><hr></div><p>But where’s here, and when is it? The year is 2013 and the place is <em>Boise, Idaho</em> (coming - running away? - from San Diego, California).</p><p>Power finds himself with a <em>brain </em>that can <em>think faster than</em> [he] <em>can but not fast enough</em>, and with thoughts that he’s sure would <em>lock </em>[him] <em>up </em>if spoken out loud. </p><p>He flees to his younger memories, where life was as fun as can be, going out hunting for snakes (too dangerous? - maybe that’s part of the fun), going out camping and swimming in lakes. </p><p>Produced alongside <strong>Jeremy Park</strong>, the song is delicate yet cadences the dreamy beat it also conveys in the lyrics: you can instantly imagine Powers reminiscing his younger years, and when the drum beat comes in you get his thoughts piling up and exploding, not too loud to make a scene, though.</p><div><hr></div><p>Maybe we’ll come back to talk about one of Youth Lagoon’s last LPs, but for now, let’s hope he’s still having fun, like when he was <em>17</em>.</p><div class="captioned-image-container"><figure><a class="image-link image2 is-viewable-img" target="_blank"
