You can be anyone you want when you dream.

Why dream of being a soldier, on the “ streets of Birkenau” ? Or of being a Spaniard in Spain “ buried with [your] marionettes” ?

Maybe because you built a balustrade with angles and corners unparalleled, and you cannot keep safe those you care about, all you are left to do is to “ travel without seatbelts on / To live this close to death ”.

The Decemberists are an indie folk band from Portland, Oregon. They formed in 2000 when Colin Meloy (vocals and guitar) met with Nate Query (bass) and Jenny Conlee (keyboards). Soon came and went Ezra Holbrook , then Rachel Blumberg , then John Moen (all on drums).

Here I Dreamt I Was an Architect is the second track on Castaways and Cutouts (2002), their first studio album.

The Decemberists

The rhythm is plain drums and keyboards, with some lines of bass to make volume. Meloy sings like he is in some sense only playing a part (multiple parts, when you count the soldier, the spaniard, the vagabond).

Here I Dreamt I Was an Architect Castaways and Cutouts (Hush Records, 2002)

And after a while, when you “ try one” , and “ try two” , you end up winning (and someone loses), and you make end meet: be them angles of your barricade, or a relationship with a “ soiled […] girlfriend” who “ furrow[ s]” like a “ lioness” .

“ Guess it always comes down to” the way you live (“ close to death” ).
