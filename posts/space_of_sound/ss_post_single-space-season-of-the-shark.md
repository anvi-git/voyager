#4 in Summer Sun, released in 2003. </p><p>Yo La Tengo was already 20 years old, having been formed in <strong>1984</strong> in Hoboken, New Jersey.</p><div><hr></div><p>Sometimes happens that a song behind?”</em>), those created by the sun when you step outside, and those that reach you when you’re among four walls. </p><p>Shadows as reflections, sometimes not perfect ones, in which we dump our insecurities, casting them away not realizing they are what we reflect, nothing more yet nothing less.</p><p>As soon as you realize that, you could use them to connect with others, to realize that “<em>they’re not as bad as they appear”</em>, and that if “<em>you need someone to help you through?”, “someone to take questions for you”, </em>you can count on one another. </p><p>And “<em>no matter how much out there scares you so</em>”, the shadows on the wall are like sharks: they seem scary for a while, then the sun sets, the season of the shark ends, and you end up being <em>alone to unwind</em>.</p><p>Kaplan’s voice is at once silky and sharp, smooth and decisive: his performance is aided by McNew’s keyboards and Hubley’s drums, to create a dreamy and warm atmosphere, a well composed lullaby that helps go through the periods when you sink so low, you even “<em>blame the sun”</em> for “<em>the shadows on the wall”</em>. </p><p>It helps remember that sometimes the only
