Please, remember me happily
“ Do my best to make a drawing of God and Lucifer ”: as you may have grasped solely from this line, it’s not easy to write something linear out of this song.
It’s a long one, full of words, images, metaphors, and recurring themes of the “great American song”.
The project was formed in 2002 by Samuel Ervin Beam (Chapin, South Carolina), with an handed four-track recorder and all the instruments played by himself. The song I am talking about here, recorded at his home studio in Florida, is one of the last songs in the All Around the Well (2009, Sub-Pop) LP, made of rare and previously unreleased songs.
![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/beabebc2-1a3c-449a-a6c6-2158dd58173c_880x1100.jpeg)
Iron & Wine can be considered “ americana ”: Beam blends folk sounds and indie introspection, and his songs often display idyllic landscapes and personal struggles. There is a sense of spirituality in his songs, and The Trapeze Swinger does not fall short of this.
![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/1320af09-04de-4a11-a294-3d5b151c979e_1000x1000.jpeg)
The song was initially published as the soundtrack of a movie called “In Good Company”, and it’s about someone who’s in between life and afterlife, or maybe they’re already dead and only looking back.
Beam asks to remember him “ happily” , “ fondly” , to remember the morning after “ Halloween” when he was “ ashamed” , maybe to have forgotten someone he was with that night.
And how many people we forget only to remember them when they are not here with us anymore!
Beam acts as someone who asks to be remembered in a dream, as a dream of youth: now he’s like a trapeze swinger, floating in a fleeting moment in the air as between life and death.
But there are not only good memories, there is also “ misery” and rejection, on and around the circus with someone the “act” could not last anymore; and now life has ended, he finds himself in front of the “ pearly gates” .
The song features in his release version Beam who plays guitar and performs the layered vocals; in the instrumental part at the end there is what seems to be a flute.
Stripped down, this song is a man asking not to be forgotten.
Every human could relate, if you ask me.
The trapeze act was wonderful
But never meant to last
See you next time!
