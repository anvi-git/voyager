his mother isn't packaged, modified somehow, if it will ever fade.</p><p>And his mother responds to him again, once more compares him to a bird, asks him again why he's crying, apologizes for having disappeared.</p><p>And then come the formalities, the hospital's request about how to treat his mother's body, the strange effect of dressing a dead body.</p><p>His mother speaks to him again, to give him one last piece of advice, to live his life to the fullest, to do it while he can.</p><p>Few songs are as heartbreaking as this one, few encapsulate in a sweet and delicate voice the entirety of emotions that such a loss entails.</p><p>The version that closes the record has a choir at the end that, repeating "We're all gonna die", the mother's caring warning, builds an emotional outro: listen
