fade me away,

I won’t ever be the same

One must love multi-instrumentalists. Recording your songs multiple times not just to score them in a decent way, but to record one instrument at a time. All of that without being carried away too easily by the fact that is your music - and only yours.

Bathing Beach is the second EP by Novo Amor , moniker of Ali John Meredith-Lacey , an artist from Wales. This record came out in 2017, while his first songs where recorded in 2014.

Lacey sings in a “profound falsetto”. This may sound counter-intuitive, so let me explain what I mean: it is similar to the way Justin Vernon sings in his first works under Bon Iver (which is a good thing, if you ask me - I didn’t explain what I meant? Don’t worry, soon we’ll talk about him).

Carry Me opens the record, “ full of aching ” and with a finger-picked guitar.

And how could one carry a doubt? “ Hollowed” , “ borrowed” , “ shadowed” , the only thing you’re left to do is to follow “ every other shade ” and to never “ stray” in “ your love”, a love that “never came” .

Colourway should be played at dusk. But “ I don’t know where we went” , “ dear” , so we should be glad to be “ coloured in” again, me and you altogether.

And beware, that love can also be another person, but that person could “ lock” you “ in […] heaven” . But you’ll always remember them, for the “ wave” they “ have made” .

![](https://substack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com/public/images/1f6938f7-3be1-4078-a0af-890d105b3b44_1000x1000.jpeg)

Anchor is a chord away to be disrupted by pain (listen to the first seconds and you’ll get why). There is an ocean, a storm, circles leading nowhere, while you search for “ someone else ”. There is a “ ship” , that’s coming for you and me, for us, and we’ll create a sea with our “ tears ” where “ I’ll swim ”.

And while you’ll swim in circles, I’ll need you to stay near our boat, so please “ anchor up to me, love ”.

The record ends with Embody me, which is a prayer you should learn by heart for those nights in life when you’re all alone, longing for someone, when your “body needs” their “body heat”. You’ll be in a “ decorated bed” , “ separated [not only] in your head”.

That someone that stays in agony when not with you will come back, and you will ask “ Oh, where’ve you been ” and will answer that he was navigating “ every present that you had” , where you are not a “ wreck” .

This record is so intimate it could have been composed inside an oak tree, but there is an ocean in it, and it certainly feels like that when you listen to these songs one after another.
