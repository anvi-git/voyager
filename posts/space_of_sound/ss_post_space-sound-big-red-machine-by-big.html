Well, ya better not fuck this up

Big Red Machine was an idea before it became a project. It was 2008 and MySpace was still a thing, and Aaron Dessner of The National contacted Justin Vernon of Bon Iver and proposed to write a song for Dark Was the Night, a record for the Red Hot Organization. The song was self-titled.

The two of them began a collaboration that ten years later led to this first record, the self-titled Big Red Machine (2018). The two artists produced this LP, along with Brad Cook and Jonathan Low .

Big Red Machine, Big Red Machine (PEOPLE / ‎Jagjaguwar‎, 2018)

Deep Green opens the record, and the sound is so well engineered that you feel, it is engineered . J.T. Bates on drums, Dessner on virtually every other instrument, Vernon on vocals (along with Lisa Hannigan ) and on guitar, and you get a two-to-three men band that seems to arrive on Earth from Alpha Centauri, having collected every experience from every planet in between. They’re not aliens though (“ not amounting to your mother or your father ”) and they’re not here to teach: I mean, they do teach, but “ when you teach, you better teach ‘em to share ”.

Gratitude is even more layered if you ask me, with a wall of drum machine and bass pedal that hits you, and a performance from Vernon that’s so visceral (even more so in a live performance). Also Bates on drums is so good at arranging a sound that’s more and more changing throughout the song, and it’s a joy to see him smile.

Justin Vernon (left) and Aaron Dessner (right)

Lyla is so funny, so disciplined in making you move off your couch and dance wihtout knowing how, it’s moving (“ I’m landing ”).

Air Stryp is a brief trip in an acid session that’s “ fucking up your classic ”, with a beautiful and unexpected ending:

in the light of the room / in an old canoe

what’s a girl to do? / in a world like you

Hymnostic is a farewell song, but the record is not done with you. It justs suggests you to “lay down right beside me?”.

New songs

that are old songs for us

Would you trust someone that is only “ an apparition” , but swears to “ haunt you” , to “ hold you” ? What if they are “ a tree” ? Maybe you should, at least someone will be “ singing for your health ”.

Forest Green would have been great it were instrumental, but it’s great anyway. “ More and more time” in those 5 minutes and 49 seconds lingers for answers ( How I’m gonna get you outta my mind?), remembers carpenteering work (“ Ya remember when we took apart your closet? ”) and in the end succumbs.

OMDB is a song built on accumulating drums and bass interactions, and to shape Vernon’s voice through Chris Messina ’s engineered vocoder (the Messina ). It’s treat to watch Vernon’s performance on this song live. The instrumental part in the middle of the song it’s worth as much as the entire record, if you ask me. The end? Even more so. If you have to pick just one song (why, though?) pick this one here. “Over My Dead Body.”

People Lullaby , as the name suggests, is a lullaby. Shut the lights, put this song on and enter in another dimension of acoustic feeling: you’re touching grass that’s thousands of kilometers away, you’re in the forests near Long Pond, water is streaming from some river in the background. “ Use your [lover’s] heart to calm [your] bed ”.

Big Red Machine, Big Red Machine (PEOPLE / ‎Jagjaguwar‎ 2018)

I Won’t Run From It feels like the view you get after a night in a cave, when you climb to the exit and look on a bright new day. And “ maybe you know, or maybe you don’t” , that sometimes you have to take things in your hands, “ took the matter to house” , “ to mouth” , and you have to go “ fishing in a harbor ”.

Melt is where the record ends, although “ we wanted to keep” the songs “ on [our] stay on our sea” a bit more. But sometimes it’s a struggle, a debacle.

Well, you are who you are

The record has ended, I hope these almost 47 minutes stuck with you.

When you don’t know what to do, maybe it’s safe, wise, and sweet to yourself to just “ follow your feet” .

I gave you all of my time

See you next time!
