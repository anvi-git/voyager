Sufjan Stevens probably need no introduction.

This is what I meant to write in the first draft of this newsletter, then I thought that this project is to make people unaware of such artists be made aware.

Sufjan Stevens is an American artist, singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist from Detroit, Michigan.

Sufjan Stevens in 2023

His debut album is “ A Sun Came ”, published on Asthmatic Kitty Records in 2000. By then, he published more than 10 studio albums, and several among compilation albums, live albums and EPs.

At the end of the piece there is the live for the album, recorded at The North Charleston Performing Arts Center, in Charleston, South Carolina.

Carrie & Lowell was initially published in 2015, as the seventh studio album by Stevens, and was then published again for the 10th anniversary of the release, on May 30, 2025.

the cover of Carrie & Lowell - 10th anniversary edition (Asthmatic Kitty Records)

And I don’t know where to begin

Death With Dignity is a delicate guitar and Stevens' voice. Stevens speaks to his silence, but " I don't know where to begin ".

He talks about oases in the desert, lush fields, but for a man without strength, what is there to do? And what is that song that is sung to the dead?

Stevens speaks to his mother, with whom he has a complicated relationship, but to whom he is close in the hours preceding her death.

After all, " every road leads to an end ".

The only thing I want to believe

Should Have Known Better is a sequence of regrets, which Stevens realizes after having removed his " shroud ". Because with that on, you can't see clearly what's important, you can't grieve, there's no time for it, and he remembers: " Nothing can be changed ".

Stevens is afraid of his feelings: he says it, he sings it, he would just like to be a "relief". But he is not the " captain of [his] feelings ".

And even if nothing can be changed, or perhaps precisely because of this, Stevens has a regret: "I should have wrote a letter". But it wouldn't have been enough, because it would have told of an "empty feeling".

He receives light from his niece, his brother's daughter: " the beauty that she brings, illumination ".

I’m just a ghost you walk right through

All of Me Wants All of You is a poignant song about a complicated but human, deeply human romantic relationship, or perhaps it only speaks of a casual encounter. Nevertheless, it remains a very sweet love song about the sensuality of bodies, about their force of attraction that doesn't really exist, but that we feel as if it were so (and if it weren't so, it doesn't matter, someone else will do it for us). Even though in the end, if things change, from " all of me wants all of you " to " all of me thinks less of you " is just a moment.

Now, how did this happen?

Drawn to the Blood is a continuous questioning (there are many questions that no one knows how to answer, in this album) of how all this happened, and what our fault is when something occurs that we (don't) want. It's also a song full of biblical references, between Delilah, Elijah, a dragon, a "winged dove".

Eugene talks about the place where Stevens lived with his mother (Carrie) and stepfather (Lowell). It speaks of loneliness, of his childhood memories, of his mother's death, of the fact that now "I'm drunk and afraid", of his being a singer, wondering if the songs he writes and plays and sings - won't they listen to them?

We’re all going to die.

I'll talk about Fourth of July regarding "Version 4", the new version in this album reissue, just under 14 minutes long (the first was just under five minutes).

It's a conversation between Sufjan Stevens and his dying mother.

Stevens speaks of an illness that spread like a fever, remembers that his mother died one night, like a firefly.

He wonders what he could have ever done to bring her back, compares her to a sky crossed by fireworks, wonderful but ephemeral.

And his mother responds to him, almost reproaches him (" you do enough talk "), asks him in a sweet tone why he's crying, if he's learned anything from the fires, which explain with clear images that things fade away, people like trees, their ashes like fireworks.

And for the first time she states something obvious, which no one really thinks about: " we're all gonna die ".

Stevens speaks again now, wonders if the memory he has of his mother isn't packaged, modified somehow, if it will ever fade.

And his mother responds to him again, once more compares him to a bird, asks him again why he's crying, apologizes for having disappeared.

And then come the formalities, the hospital's request about how to treat his mother's body, the strange effect of dressing a dead body.

His mother speaks to him again, to give him one last piece of advice, to live his life to the fullest, to do it while he can.

Few songs are as heartbreaking as this one, few encapsulate in a sweet and delicate voice the entirety of emotions that such a loss entails.

The version that closes the record has a choir at the end that, repeating "We're all gonna die", the mother's caring warning, builds an emotional outro: listen to it one evening, late at night, don't do anything else, and you'll find yourself in places perhaps a bit sad, maybe a bit haunting, maybe not completely pleasant, but that will surely know how to cradle you.

I wanna save you from your sorrow

The Only Thing starts with throwing oneself off a cliff with a car, then being "buried where you are found", wondering if he really cares - about continuing to live - if "all of this" (life, his career, his affections, his memories?) doesn't please him.

He answers himself that there is a reason, and places his faith in it. But then again, "should I tear my heart out now?", because everything "brings me back to you". And so for what I see, so for what I feel, for what I touch?

What is life if our life becomes another person, when that person is no longer there?

such a long time ago

Carrie & Lowell is a dialogue between Stevens and himself, with a double voice that continues throughout the entire track. And there are also two characters who enter the song, the two parental figures (who however didn't both raise him): Stevens' stepfather and his mother.

The song is a succession of images, (" cottage grove shade ", " covered bridge ", " head on the floorboards "), that bring Stevens back to a childhood that wasn't exactly pleasant, but also of trips taken in the state of Oregon, whose state animal is the meadowlark, which is mentioned in the first verse.

The dual nature of this song is also felt in the rhythm, in the continuous bouncing back and forth of the guitars, and of the drum-pedal.

What can be said of my heart

John My Beloved is a prayer, metaphorical to a lover, and real to Jesus Christ. The track opens with Stevens at the bar, drunk or just awkward, talking to the " beloved of John ", that is Jesus, apologizing for reading his word as if it were a poem.

And the song speaks of mystery, of life after death, of this one before it, of loneliness and greed, of awareness (" I am a man with a heart that offends / With its lonely and greedy demands "), of the fact that in a certain sense we are (already) dead, of the fact that the dove is perhaps the Christian version of the myth of Icarus.

The track is packed full of Scripture citations, and has an extremely high density of meanings; Stevens' delicate voice, the gentle piano touch make this song almost a confession (which perhaps it is, after all, because it ends invoking Jesus, to be "near me").

Fuck me, I’m falling apart

No Shade in the Shadows of the Cross is the account of how Stevens lived with his mother's passing, of the things he did and those he felt like doing, all mediated by the religious sentiment that drives and penetrates him.

He speaks of the way he felt he had to self-destruct in order to feel close to her, to emulate her self-destructive behaviour in life; he speaks of the brief moments of awareness after episodes of self-harm, substance and alcohol abuse. It's a heartbreaking lament, funereal in melody and rhythm, that delivers the most human and wounded version of Stevens.

Lord, touch me with lightning

Blue Bucket of Gold is the album's closing song (not in the 10th anniversary version, which also contains demos and new versions of old tracks). It's a song in which Stevens searches for his mother like one searches for a treasure (the blue bucket of gold is a treasure rumored to be present in Oregon).

He would like to be part of someone's life, he would like something to admire, something to love, he desires a conversion: he asks the Lord to strike him like lightning, like Paul of the Scriptures.

The song, and side A of the record, close with angelic vocalisations, but at the same time lugubrious, divine but heartbreaking.

Death With Dignity (Demo) , Should Have Known Better (Demo) , The Only Thing (Demo) , Eugene (Demo) are less structured versions than those present on side A, but Stevens' voice strikes even more; the last one features the collaboration of S. Carey, among other things a member of Bon Iver.

Oh, will wonders ever cease?

Mystery of Love (Demo) , a song composed by Stevens for Luca Guadagnino 's movie Call Me By Your Name , is published here in a version similar to the previous demos: voice and guitar. It speaks of Hephaestion and Alexander the Great separated by death, of not being able to find anyone else. It's a not particularly happy love song, of that love that dare not speak its name.

But have you heard the story of my mother’s fate?

Wallowa Lake Monster (Version 2) is the account of the way Stevens imagines (or remembers) taking care of a mother, like a heroic undertaking, while another battle was being fought: that of the mother against a monster (abuse?) which in the end, this dragon, got the better of her. " When the dragon submerged we knew she had died ". The instrumental outro is incredible.

Fourth of July (Version 4) closes the record. We talked about it previously in the piece. Listen to it again, if you want.
