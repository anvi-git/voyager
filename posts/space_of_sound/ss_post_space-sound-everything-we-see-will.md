<p><strong><a href="https://open.spotify.com/artist/249C09TWxdBKs7Z5BN2MU6?si=YnQ2BOKxQw6B7RPq-8Il3A">At The End of Times, Nothing</a></strong> is not just a <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/astro-ph/9701131">cosmologically</a> accurate prospect, it's also the title of an instrumental project by <strong>Sven Karlsson</strong>, a Swedish musician and producer.</p><p><strong><a href="https://open.spotify.com/album/0H0yMqOQ1XMYe5RUaSX9so?si=q0Jf5xeAQA-DWVwidmcL8w">Everything We See Will Die</a></strong> is the second EP produced under this name, and it was published in 2016 with the label <em>Epidemic Sound</em>; the songs are indeed instrumental, composed with electric guitars, drum beats and impetuous crescendos.</p><iframe class="spotify-wrap album" data-attrs="{&quot;image&quot;:&quot;https://i.scdn.co/image/ab67616d0000b273fe108d08e37b8be406de673d&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;Everything We See Will Die&quot;,&quot;subtitle&quot;:&quot;At The End Of Times, Nothing&quot;,&quot;description&quot;:&quot;Album&quot;,&quot;url&quot;:&quot;https://open.spotify.com/album/0H0yMqOQ1XMYe5RUaSX9so&quot;,&quot;belowTheFold&quot;:false,&quot;noScroll&quot;:false}" src="https://open.spotify.com/embed/album/0H0yMqOQ1XMYe5RUaSX9so" frameborder="0" gesture="media" allowfullscreen="true" allow="encrypted-media" data-component-name="Spotify2ToDOM"></iframe><p><em>The Ground After a Summer Rain</em> has a title with a cyclical meaning connected to the last track (we'll discuss this at the end); after a timid entrance of the drums accompanying the electric guitar, the two chase each other, growing more and more until they reach the point of explosion, doing so and leaving the same instruments on the road, tired, destroyed, tested by the effort.</p><div><hr></div><p><em>The Sky is No Limit</em> starts slowly, and here the drums support the rhythm of the guitar, until the opposite happens, and it's the beat that remains confined by the small guitar riffs.</p><p class="button-wrapper" data-attrs="{&quot;url&quot;:&quot;https://spaceofsound.substack.com/p/space-sound-everything-we-see-will?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email&utm_content=share&action=share&quot;,&quot;text&quot;:&quot;Share&quot;,&quot;action&quot;:null,&quot;class&quot;:null}" data-component-name="ButtonCreateButton"><a class="button primary" href="https://spaceofsound.substack.com/p/space-sound-everything-we-see-will?utm_source=substack&utm_medium=email&utm_content=share&action=share"><span>Share</span></a></p><p><em>Last Sunrise in the Wasteland</em> is in anxious anticipation of fulfilling itself from the moment the drums enter the scene: and when it does explode with a roar that leaves few seconds before moving on to the next, <em>Once Every 20 Years</em>, which is perhaps the most classic of all, almost restoring a sense of normality that ultimately, once every twenty years, is acceptable.
