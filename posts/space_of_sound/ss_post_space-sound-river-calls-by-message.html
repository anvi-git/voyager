My lungs on fire

Jerome Alexander is a British composer and multi-instrumentalist. He published under the moniker Message to Bears his first EP ( EP1 ) in 2007, and on June 2025 has published River Calls , under Lost Birds Records .

River Calls starts with track #1 with the same title, and immediately comes to mind Alexander’s other works ( Maps in particular, if you ask me).

There’s electronica, and a constant beat, rain is falling it seems, echoes are travelling through air, and Alexander’s vocalisations guide us through this forest, one layer after the other, to reach the deeps of his intent.

It’s a wonderful first track, that sets the mood for the entire record, and makes it worth the whole run.

After that comes Disquiet , where I recall echoes of I Know You Love to Fall, from the record Maps.

When I first heard this song, I travelled back in time, and arrived to a place in space where I felt grateful to Alexander to bring those sounds to life: it’s an emotional ladder which feels on the brink to explode and make you tremble, but then lulls you into a delicate outro.

Open Lungs is the only song properly “sung” in the EP, and what a song that is! My lungs are on fire , Alexander sings, and that is due to the moon colliding, creating a hole, now filled with snow and stone. Whatever planet Alexander is guiding us to, I’d be joyful to burn my lungs to sing this song (or maybe not, but let’s assume for a bit that I’m a poet - Alexander surely is - and that that it’s just a metaphor).

Half Light closes the EP, but the end does not come too soon. It still gives us goosebumps thanks to its layering delicate sounds and electronic-filled beats which resound now more than ever a heartbeat, making full circle with the first song on this record.

River Calls is another great album by British composer Jerome Alexander, and I’m grateful to him for all those nights I spent listening to these sounds, travelling standing still.
