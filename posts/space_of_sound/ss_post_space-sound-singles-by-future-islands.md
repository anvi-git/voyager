<blockquote><p><em><strong>sun in the morning</strong></em></p><p><em><strong>my sun every morning</strong></em></p></blockquote><p>Some songs are buried deep and then come out; it’s the case with a song we will talk about below on this album by <strong><a href="https://www.future-islands.com/">Future Islands</a></strong>. The record is <em><a href="https://futureislands.bandcamp.com/album/singles">Singles (2014)</a></em> and it’s the third of the band from Greenville, North Carolina. </p><p><strong>Samuel T. Herring</strong> (vocals), <strong>William Cashion</strong> (bass and guitar), and <strong>Gerrit Welmers</strong> (keyboards, programming, guitar) formed the band in 2006, with other members who later left the group. In 2014 <strong>Mike Lowry</strong> (<strong>Lake Trout</strong>, <strong>Mt. Royal</strong>, <strong>The Far Field</strong>) joined the band (drums).</p><div><hr></div><p>Future Islands sound like wind through clouds, a light cone outside a station, a warm blanket on the first nights of winter.</p><iframe class="spotify-wrap album" src="https://open.spotify.com/embed/album/26bhmgVpg5E5HyU4K1BYMc" frameborder="0" gesture="media" allowfullscreen="true" allow="encrypted-media"></iframe><p>The LP opens with Herring’s voice already “<em>waiting on</em> [us]” in <em>Seasons (Waiting on You)</em>, and we immediately learn that his voice will be a fundamental part of this record. The synth atmosphere is poised in this first song and comes out more predominantly in <em>Spirit</em>, which you could experience while crossing multiple times the revolving doors of a discotheque restroom. This song is an invitation to be more than words and strength, but also more than […] kind. </p><p>Herring almost cries or shrieks here: it’s a dynamic song, as he described in an <a href="https://consequence.net/2014/04/future-islands-looking-toward-the-past-present-and-future/">interview</a>; that “<em>create</em>[s] <em>tension and bring</em>[s] <em>people close, then blast</em>[s] <em>them with a message</em>”.</p><p><em>Sun in the Morning</em> cuts you through like the first sunlight after a long night in which you had almost any rest; the sound surrounds you, Herring sings such sweet verses and you wish to pass them on to someone next to you. The only thing is you would end up waking her up: how lucky those who know that after all, “<em>she’s always coming home”</em>!</p><p><em>Doves</em> has a warmongery for peace in the first two verses, then it’s a repeated beg sung by Herring and sustained by the band; <em>Back in the Tall Grass</em> opens with a firm bass and drums, then echoes into a song entirely devoted to “<em>thinking of you</em>”. </p><div class="captioned-image-container"><figure><a class="image-link image2 is-viewable-img" target="_blank" href="https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cb1bbab-32c9-44b4-bba7-7ba1133acf3e_1200x1200.jpeg"><div class="image2-inset"><picture><source type="image/webp" srcset="https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cb1bbab-32c9-44b4-bba7-7ba1133acf3e_1200x1200.jpeg 424w, https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cb1bbab-32c9-44b4-bba7-7ba1133acf3e_1200x1200.jpeg 848w, https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cb1bbab-32c9-44b4-bba7-7ba1133acf3e_1200x1200.jpeg 1272w, https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cb1bbab-32c9-44b4-bba7-7ba1133acf3e_1200x1200.jpeg 1456w" sizes="100vw"><img src="https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2cb1bbab-32c9-44b4-bba7-7ba1133acf3e_1200x1200.jpeg" alt="Image"></picture></a></figure></div><p><em>A Song for Our Grandfathers</em> has a similar opening, with bass and keyboards; it feels like a constant reminder of the one “<em>grandfather looking over me</em>” could have given; Light House is a little gem, I really think this could span different generations and reach them all. Maybe the great sound, the strong and sweet voice of one docile Herring, “<em>when I showed you the dark</em>” that gets me everytime, or the little growls in the voice in the second half: it’s a go-to for a great summary of Future Islands’ sound.</p><p><em>Like the moon</em> feels like when you walk on a beach at night, the drum machine as the waves, and the bass as passing light from a lighthouse, with someone you say “<em>I can call you friend / But I won’t be around</em>”. </p><p><em>Fall From Grace</em> is nothing like its title: when I listened again for this piece I wondered “This could become almost shoegaze, why it didn’t?”, then the second part arrived and I corrected myself.  “<em>Was it all inside of me?</em>” No, the song goes exactly there, I caught nothing fancy that was there but my memories. </p><p>You’ve been to the beach, you’ve laid on the bed at dawn looking for the first sunlight, you’ve gathered with family in the fields to sing about your forefathers; but “<em>don’t you believe what you’ve been told</em> / <em>people lie, people love, people go</em>”. </p><p>And that is indeed true: you should not even listen to me. “<em>Wait for the morning</em>”, going to the beach “<em>staring at the sea</em>”, and finally “<em>ask</em>[…] [yourself] <em>for peace</em>". As Herring sings, “<em>beauty lies in every soul</em>”.</p><blockquote><p><em><strong>my star of the evening</strong></em></p><p><em><strong>my moon always beaming</strong></em></p></blockquote><div id="youtube2-upPl9mZW_zw" class="youtube-wrap"><div class="youtube-inner"><iframe src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/upPl9mZW_zw?rel=0&amp;autoplay=0&amp;showinfo=0&amp;enablejsapi=0" frameborder="0" loading="lazy" gesture="media" allow="autoplay; fullscreen" allowautoplay="true" allowfullscreen="true" width="728" height="409"></iframe></div></div><div><hr></div><p>If you liked this, you could share this project with others.
